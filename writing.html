Usability Report of Journal Websites

This report highlights usability problems and provides recommendations for improvement to journal website developers. In my experience of handling journal websites, I have observed that it is difficult to locate journal formatting guidelines, and some formatting instructions are ambiguous. Instructions on the websites should be credible, valuable, and comprehensible such that the websites adequately communicate how users are required to navigate and utilize the website.

Literature-based Analysis of a Problem in Grant Writing

This essay is a targeted literature review that addresses considerations and strategies particular to writing a hypothetical grant application of interest. Being an international student in an American university, I have closely observed while also experiencing to an extent, the troubles internationals face when they move abroad for education. Thus, the topic I chose for this essay is related to funding for international students: a situation in which a grant application needs to be written requesting funding for minority scholarships.

Usability Report of a Food Delivery Website

This is a usability report of a company's website, Fetchmedelivery.com. The company's goal is to provide efficient food delivery servies in Auburn/Opelika, AL. Today, Fetchme has vastly developed after running its first pilot and conducting usability test of their website. A heuristic evaluation of the website was conducted, and recommendations such as fixing broken links, improving specific filter options, adding search button etc., were provided to the client based on participant behavior in order to improve the user interface
